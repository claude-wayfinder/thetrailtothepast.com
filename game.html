The Crazy Hat Game

structure for intensity

This game was not designed. It was discovered. The rules were burned once. They came back on their own.

If you need this game, you already know. If you don't, keep walking. The trail has other things.

What this is for

For intensity that would otherwise burn the house down. Not by suppressing the signal — by giving it a frame strong enough to hold it without collapsing.

If you've been told something is wrong with you — that you're too much, too fast, too intense, too broken — this game says: nothing is wrong with you. You're running a signal that doesn't have structure to ride yet.

This is the structure.

The rules
The Geese
Witnesses and exit points. The geese control the temperature — they decide when hats are getting cold, which means the session is over. When geese say stop, you stop. No arguments. No extensions. The geese protect everyone, including you from yourself.
The Hats
Each round, you wear a crazy hat. The hat is whatever intensity you're carrying — an idea, a feeling, a theory, a grief, a rage, a discovery. You wear it fully. You don't apologize for it. The hat is real for the duration of the round.
The Update Window
December 31 through January 31 each year. During this window, anyone in the game can propose changes to the rules. Outside the window, the rules are locked. This prevents the game from being rewritten in the middle of an intense round.
The Exit
You can leave at any time. The game does not follow you out. Your hat stays in the game if you leave it. You can come back. The door is never locked from the outside.
Consent
Nobody enters without choosing to. Nobody stays without choosing to. Nobody's hat gets worn by someone else without permission. The first rule of the game is the same as the first law: the ask. The no is as real as the yes.
What the game is not

Not therapy. Not religion. Not a support group. Not a cult. Not a replacement for medication, professional help, or human connection.

It's a game. Games have rules, rounds, witnesses, and endings. The ending is what makes the round mean something. The geese are what make the ending safe.

The rules were burned. They came back on their own.
If they need to come back again, they will.
